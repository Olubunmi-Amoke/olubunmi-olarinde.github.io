Topic: Algorithmic Bias in Symptom Interpretation for Marginalized Health Communities

This presentation explores how natural language processing models used in healthcare may misinterpret expressions of pain from marginalized communities, especially Sickle Cell Disease patients. Using philosophical and technical frameworks, it outlines the ethical risks of misrepresentation, epistemic injustice, and systemic harm.

Abstract

Many NLP tools deployed in clinical settings rely on standard expressions of symptoms and emotions. For communities whose voices have historically been excluded or misunderstood, this creates a risk of algorithmic misinterpretation. This project centers Sickle Cell Disease patients and explores how participatory design, relational ethics, and epistemic justice can guide more equitable AI development.
