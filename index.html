About Me

I am a machine learning enthusiast and graduate student in the Master of Data Science program at Eastern University, PA. I am passionate about using data-driven solutions to solve real-world problems across domains such as health, education, business, and social impact. I care deeply about building systems that are not only effective and scalable but also fair, human-centered, and ethically responsible.

Career-wise, I’m drawn to applied AI roles that blend technical problem-solving with public impact: whether in research, product development, or mission-driven innovation. My academic background is multidisciplinary, and I bring a unique blend of analytical rigor, creative thinking, and lived insight to the table.

Outside of data science, I am a vocal coach and a music performer who still sneaks in harmonies during long coding sessions! Whether it’s teaching music theory or analyzing data, I enjoy exploring creative ways to connect technology with everyday life.

This site includes my ethics capstone presentation, The Model Doesn’t Speak My Pain, which explores algorithmic bias in symptom interpretation using real-world and philosophical frameworks. It also features selected projects including my data science capstone, The Smart Sickle Cell Diary App, a web-based application which explores symptom tracking and insight generation for Sickle Cell Disease management.

Fun fact: I once ran a summer music camp with kids aged 6 to 13: equal parts chaos and pure joy!
