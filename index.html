About Me

I am a machine learning enthusiast and graduate student in the Master of Data Science program at Eastern University, PA. I am particularly drawn to projects that sit at the intersection of data science, public health, and ethical AI. I care deeply about creating systems that not only function well but are also fair, human-centered, and socially responsible.

Career-wise, I’m drawn to applied AI roles that blend technical problem-solving with public impact: whether in research, product development, or mission-driven innovation. My academic background is multidisciplinary, and I bring a unique blend of analytical rigor, creative thinking, and lived insight to the table.

Outside of data science, I am a vocal coach and a music performer who still sneaks in harmonies during long coding sessions! Whether it’s teaching music theory or analyzing data, I enjoy exploring creative ways to connect technology with everyday life. This site showcases selected projects, including my capstone project—The Smart Sickle Cell Diary App—which explores symptom tracking and insight generation for Sickle Cell Disease management.

Fun fact: I once ran a summer music camp with kids aged 6 to 13: equal parts chaos and pure joy!
